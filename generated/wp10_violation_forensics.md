# WP10c keep-out violation forensics (read-only replay)

Forensic classification of every keep-out-violating run in the committed
`generated/wp5_campaign_runs.csv`, per spec v5 section 6 WP10c. Produced by
`tools/forensics/make_forensics.py` (Python 3 stdlib, deterministic, R6/R9);
regenerated by `tools/regenerate_all.sh`. No campaign or control-law code
was modified or executed: the tool replays the committed campaign's
deterministic draw stream and abort-coast physics from the pinned seeds.

## 1. Replay recipe (WP10c completion criterion)

- Per-run seed: `SplitMix64(master_seed XOR FNV1a64(catalog), run_index)`
  with `master_seed = 0x5AD5C0DECAFE2026` (campaign.hpp); the run seeds in
  the forensics table below equal the committed `run_seed` column.
- Draw stream: `std::mt19937_64(run_seed)`, 53-bit uniforms in (0, 1],
  explicit Box-Muller with spare caching, in the exact draw order of
  `run_one_mission` (campaign.cpp).
- Abort physics: Clohessy-Wiltshire drift-null impulse capped at 2.0 m/s
  (mission.cpp `compute_safe_abort`), RK4 coast at dt = 8 s over 1 orbital
  period of the catalog altitude (the campaign's coarsened screen,
  main_campaign.cpp), keep-out radius 200 m, departure standoff 400 m.
- To reproduce: `python3 tools/forensics/make_forensics.py` from the repo
  root, or `bash tools/regenerate_all.sh build` for the full pipeline.

## 2. Replay cross-validation (gate for everything below)

- 1000/1000 committed runs replayed with ZERO mismatches across all
  replay-reachable CSV columns (run_seed, solar_factor,
  first_closing_speed, tumble_rate, dv_used, dv_remaining, removals,
  targets_attempted, gate_abort_events, kits_used, outcome), each compared
  at the CSV's own printed precision.
- 576 gate-abort events replayed; the replay independently reproduces
  exactly the committed set of 14 keep-out violations (SL-16 / Zenit-2 second stage: 7, SL-8 / Kosmos-3M second stage: 7).
- Columns NOT replayable in Python (attitude-dynamics outputs:
  sync_time_s, mission_time_s) are not used by this analysis.
- Modeled assumption, guarded: every sync attempt succeeds. The committed
  CSV contains 0 sync_timeout_events across all 1000 runs; the tool
  hard-fails if that ever changes.

## 3. Findings

**The D13 working hypothesis is REFUTED by the data.** D13 hypothesized the
primary mechanism as *capped-abort residual drift* re-entering the keep-out
sphere. In fact **all 14 violations are CLEAN aborts**: the commanded
drift-null impulse (max 0.306 m/s across the violating runs) never reaches
the 2.0 m/s cap, and the post-burn secular drift term |vy + 2 n x| is
numerically zero for every violating run (column `post_burn_drift_m_per_s`).
Capped aborts observed among the violations: 0.

**Actual mechanism -- clean-abort safety-ellipse keep-out intersection.**
The abort law nulls the drift, which yields a *bounded* relative orbit,
not necessarily a *clearing* one. The post-burn coast is the closed-form ellipse
`x(t) = x0 cos(nt)`, `y(t) = y0 - 2 x0 sin(nt)`, `z(t) = z0 cos(nt)`: a
dispersed radial offset x0 produces an along-track swing of 2|x0| about the
dispersed standoff y0, so the coast approaches the target to roughly
`|y0| - 2|x0|` (exactly: the sampled ellipse minimum, column
`analytic_ellipse_min_range_m`, which matches the RK4 sweep within the 8 s
screen resolution). Every violating run has |x0| between 62.6 m and 142.4 m
(1.6-3.6 sigma of the 40 m per-axis dispersion), and the coast minimum
occurs at a quarter orbit (x0 < 0) or three-quarter orbit (x0 > 0), exactly
as the ellipse geometry predicts. The violation is a *geometric* property
of where the abort starts, not a thruster-authority (cap) property.

Classification of all 14 violating runs:
`clean_abort_safety_ellipse_intersects_keep_out`: 14 of 14.
`capped_abort_residual_drift`: 0.
`starts_inside_keep_out`: 0.

Model scope: this analysis is [L0: linear CW coast, campaign keep-out
screen at dt = 8 s over 1 period, WP5 committed dispersion set as
configured in CampaignConfig]; it inherits every WP5 model limitation
(sensor dispersions drawn but not re-propagated; flat placeholder
Delta-v costs; see the evidence-pack inventory).

## 5. Near-miss context (WP11 input)

Of the 562 replayed non-violating aborts, the smallest clearance above the
200 m keep-out sphere is 10.8 m, and 38 aborts cleared by less than 50 m.
The margin distribution is continuous down to essentially zero: the
committed violation rate (14 of 1000 runs; see wp5_campaign_summary.md) is
the tail of a broad near-miss population, not a cluster
of isolated outliers. Any WP11 fix must therefore constrain the abort
ENTRY STATE (e.g. a reachability/ellipse-clearance check before committing
to a state, and/or choosing an abort impulse that also shapes the ellipse)
rather than only raising thruster authority: `abort_dv` authority is not
the binding constraint in any observed violation.
