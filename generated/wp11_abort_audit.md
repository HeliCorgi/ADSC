# WP11 abort audit (clearing-abort law, committed campaign)

Independent stdlib-Python replay of the committed ds-v1 campaign under
the WP11 clearing-abort law, hard-gated on reproducing the committed
`generated/wp5_campaign_runs.csv` before reporting anything. Produced by
`tools/forensics/make_forensics.py`; regenerated by
`tools/regenerate_all.sh`. See `wp10_violation_forensics.md` for the
archived legacy-law forensics that motivated WP11 (D13).

## 1. Replay cross-validation (gate for everything below)

- 1000/1000 committed runs replayed under the clearing law with ZERO
  mismatches across every replay-reachable column (run_seed,
  solar_factor, first_closing_speed, tumble_rate, dv_used, dv_remaining,
  removals, targets_attempted, gate_abort_events, kits_used, outcome,
  schema_version, dispersion_set_id, worst_abort_clearance_m), each at
  the CSV's own printed precision.
- Columns NOT replayable in Python (attitude-dynamics outputs:
  sync_time_s, mission_time_s) are not used by this audit.
- Modeled assumption, guarded: every sync attempt succeeds (0
  sync_timeout_events committed).

## 2. Keep-out result (WP11 completion criterion, R14-tagged)

- SL-16 / Zenit-2 second stage: keep-out violations **0 of 500** [L0: linear CW, dispersion
  set ds-v1, Wilson 95% upper bound 0.0076].
- SL-8 / Kosmos-3M second stage: keep-out violations **0 of 500** [L0: linear CW, dispersion
  set ds-v1, Wilson 95% upper bound 0.0076].

A zero still carries an interval (R15/WP11): the Wilson upper bound is
quoted above, never a bare zero. The legacy-law campaign at the same
seeds violated in 14 runs (see the archive); the clearing law changed
the abort, not the dispersions, so the comparison is seed-for-seed.

Reading: `Clean` = the legacy drift-null impulse already clears keep-out

violated); `RetreatHop`/`Capped` = escalations, not expected from the
campaign's standoff geometry.

The same dispersed abort states, swept over the impulse budget cap:

| abort_dv cap [m/s] | Clean | BoundedClearing | RetreatHop | Capped | violations | clearance floor [m] |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 0.5 | 562 | 21 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 20.0002 |
| 1.0 | 562 | 21 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 20.0002 |
| 2.0 | 562 | 21 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 20.0002 |
| 4.0 | 562 | 21 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 20.0002 |

Reading: thruster authority was never the binding constraint (the WP10c
finding); the trade shows the clearing law is insensitive to the cap
across 0.5-4.0 m/s for this dispersion set because the reshape and hop
impulses are small (<~0.4 m/s). `abort_dv` therefore stays at its
legacy 2.0 m/s value; no pinned number moves on account of this trade.

## 6. Replay recipe and honest limits

- Recipe: identical to the WP10c archive (section 1 there), with the
  clearing-abort law (mission.cpp `clearing_abort_for`) in place of the
  legacy law; stage math mirrored per the provenance comments in
  `tools/forensics/make_forensics.py`.
- [L0: linear CW, dispersion set ds-v1]; sensor dispersions drawn but
  not re-propagated (WP5 documented simplification); flat placeholder
  Delta-v costs; the campaign keep-out screen runs at dt = 8 s over 1
  period (main_campaign.cpp overrides).
- This audit reports the committed campaign; it changes no behavior and
  quotes no number that does not regenerate from the committed CSVs.
